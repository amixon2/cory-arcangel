Is All So Crazy, Everybody Seems So Famous



Name: This Is All So Crazy, Everybody Seems So Famous
Year: 2015
Medium: Digital art, video installations, digital animations, interactive digital experiences, and software-based artworks.
Description: Cory Arcangel's show "This Is All So Crazy, Everybody Seems So Famous" explores the contemporary concept of celebrity and its connection to digital media. Arcangel investigates the production, representation, and consumption of celebrity in contemporary culture using a variety of media, including installations, digital art, and video. He combines and reinterprets well-known cultural emblems with comedy and sarcasm, asking viewers to reevaluate the meaning of identity and renown. Incorporating aspects from popular culture and the internet, Arcangel invites visitors to connect with these issues in a lighthearted yet thought-provoking way, stimulating debate on the relevance and authenticity of celebrity in the digital age.
Exhibition: Gamec in Bergamo
Cultural Impact: A broader societal trend toward digitization and a rising infiltration of technology into daily life are reflected in Arcangel's use of technology and digital art in the show. The program examines how technology affects our relationships with art, culture, and each other through interaction, digital animation, and video.
